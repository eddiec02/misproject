1. Wordpress Optimization

This section provides you with instructions for optimizing your Wordpress installation. These steps are not required, however we highly recommend them.

1.1 Modify Upload Path

Modifying your upload path is a very beneficial modification. It keeps things nice and tidy and makes it much easier should you ever have to move your website and locate files in the future.

  1. Log into your Wordpress Dashboard.
  2. Click on Settings > Media.
  3. Scroll to the bottom and un-check the box labeled "Organize my uploads into month- and year-based folders"
  4. Save Changes.


1.2 Update Permalink Structure

This will give you SEO-friendly page links.

  1. Log into your Wordpress Dashboard.
  2. Click on Settings > Permalinks.
  3. Choose Custom Structure and type /%postname% into the provided field.
  4. Save Changes.


2. Theme Installation

Please follow these steps to install your new Wordpress Theme:

  1. Download the ZIP package from Themeforest and Unzip it.
  2. Within the unzipped folder you will see a file named Karma.zip - this is the actual Wordpress Theme.
  3. Log into your Wordpress Dashboard and click on Appearance > Themes.
  4. Next click on Install Themes then click on Upload.
  5. Click Browse then locate the file named Karma.zip and click Install Now.
  6. After the theme has installed click on Activate Theme and you are all set! Your new theme is now installed!

3. Site Options Panel

This theme comes with a very powerful Site Options panel. After the theme is installed you can access this panel by logging into the Wordpress dashboard and clicking on Appearance > Site Options. All options are very self explanitory and are covered in detail in the instructional Training Videos.


4. Menus and Navigation

You can access the Menu control panel by clicking on  Appearance > Menus. Full details are also covered in the Training Videos.

Main Menu, Footer Menu & Top Menu Setup:

  1. Log into your Wordpress dashboard and click on Appearance > Menus.
  2. Enter a Name for your Menu and click on Create Menu.
  3. Add Pages to your menu by checking off the check boxes and hitting Add to Menu
  4. To re-order the pages of your menu items simply drag and drop them into place.
  5. After your menu is completed hit Save Menu.
  6. After Your menu is saved you'll need to choose your navigation from the dropdown list under the 'Theme Locations' section.
  7. Hit save and you are all complete. Your menu is now setup.


5. Blog Setup

Setting up your Blog section is a very straightforward process. It simply entails creating a new page, choosing the proper page template and pointing Wordpress to your new Blog page. Full details are also covered in the Training Videos.

Creating the Blog page:

  1. Log into your Wordpress Dashboard.
  2. Click on Pages > Add New.
  3. Give your Page a title and choose the Blog page template.
  4. Save and rejoice! :)


Pointing Wordpress to your Blog page:

  1. Log into your Wordpress Dashboard.
  2. Click on Settings > Reading.
  3. Choose the radio button labeled A static page (select below) .
  4. Choose your Homepage for Front Page, and choose your Blog page for Posts Page.
  5. All done. Your Blog is now fully functional.
  6. You can visit the Site Options Panel for further Blog customizing. Appearance > Site Options

7. Sidebars

Creating Sidebars is an absolute breeze. Simply create the Sidebar, add your Widgets and assign the Sidebar to your chosen page. Full details are also covered in the Training Videos.

Creating a new Sidebar:

  1. Log into your Wordpress Dashboard.
  2. Click on Appearance > Sidebars.
  3. Click on + Add New Sidebar.
  4. Give your Sidebar a name and click OK. (note: the name of the sidebar is never displayed to your end users.)


Adding Widgets to a Sidebar:

  1. Log into your Wordpress Dashboard.
  2. Click on Appearance > Widgets.
  3. Locate the Sidebar you just created. It will be located toward the bottom right of your screen.
  4. Drag your desired Widgets into your Sidebar.


Assigning Sidebar to Page:

  1. Log into your Wordpress Dashboard.
  2. Click on Pages and edit your desired page. (please note that you must be using either a
    Left Sidebar or Right Sidebar page template.)
  3. Scroll down and locate the custom meta box labeled Sidebars.
  4. Select your Sidebar from the Dropdown list.
  5. Save your page and rejoice! :)

9. Gallery/Portfolio Setup

This section describes how to setup a Gallery/Portfolio page within your website. Full details are also covered in the online Training Videos.

Create a Post Category

  1. The first step is to create a post category that will be used for your gallery items.
  2. Log into the Wordpress dashboard and click on Posts > Categories.
  3. Enter a new category name and click Add New Category.
  4. You are now ready to begin creating gallery posts.

Add Posts to the Gallery

  1. From within the Wordpress dashboard click on Posts > Add New.
  2. Enter a name for the post and if you prefer, a brief description in the content section.
  3. You now need to provide the post with a "featured image" and the URL to your "full size item". The "full size item" is what the user sees when they click on the thumbnail. The full-size item can be a flash movie, youtube video, etc etc. Here are samples to show how these URL's should look:
    •  
    • Image: http://www.yourdomain.com/wp-content/uploads/project1.jpg
    • YouTube: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VKS08be78os
    • Flash: http://www.yourdomain.com/wp-content/uploads/design.swf?width=792&height=294
    • Vimeo: http://vimeo.com/8245346
    • iFrame: http://www.apple.com?iframe=true&width=850&height=500
    •  
  4. Enter the URL for the full-size item into the 'Portfolio Full Size URL' box.
  5. Add a the thumbnails by adding a 'featured image'.
  6. Repeat those steps for creating as many portoflio items as you desire.
  7. The next step is to create a Gallery Page.

Creating a Gallery Page:

  1. Create a new page by clicking on Pages > Add New.
  2. Choose your desired page template and assign it to the page you just created.
    (ie. Portfolio :: 1 Column)
  3. You now need to specify which post category to display on this page. To do so, scroll down to the the "Portfolio Category" box and choose your post categpry from the dropdown list. Save your changes.
  4. Your gallery page is now complete!
